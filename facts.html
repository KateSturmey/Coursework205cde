Fruit

/fruːt/
noun
1.the sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant that contains seed and can be eaten as food.
verb
1.(of a tree or other plant) produce fruit.

We should all eat more fruit, but do we actually know which fruits we should be eating to suit us best? Yes we know the saying, an apple a day keeps the doctor away. So lets do a little Alphabet of fruits -
A - Avocado, healthy fats that can help lower cholesterol levels when eaten instead of saturated fats.
B - Blueberries, they rank No.1 in antioxidant activity compared to 60 others. Blueberries may help lower the risk of developing age-related diseases such as Parkinson's and Alzheimer's.
C - Cranberries, a recent research has linked cranberries to the prevention of kidney stones and ulcers.
D - Dates,a good source of vitamins and minerals. Its a good source of energy, sugar and fiber.

Vegetables

vɛdʒtəb(ə)l,ˈvɛdʒɪtə-/
noun plural noun: vegetables
1. plant or part of a plant used as food, such as a cabbage, potato, turnip, or bean.

Likewise to fruit, Vegetables should be seen throughout our diet, theyre high in vitamins and minerals that help our body function.
A- Asparagus, It's loaded with nutrients, avery good source of fiber, folate, vitamins A, C, E and K.
B - Beetroot, improves blood flow and lowers blood pressure.
C - Cabbage, One of the worlds healthiest foods!, full of antioxidants and Vitamin C great for your skin too!
D - Dill, has a significant amount of vitamin A and vitamin C, as well as trace amounts of folate, iron, and manganese.
